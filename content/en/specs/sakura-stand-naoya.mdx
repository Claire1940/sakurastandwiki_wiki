<Section icon={Map} title="How to Obtain the Naoya Zen'in Spec">

Obtaining **Sakura Stand Naoya** is a multi-stage process that requires players to be cautious about their current equipped abilities. The Zen'in progression route is strictly gated; if you have another Jujutsu Kaisen Spec equipped, such as Sukuna or Itadori Yuji, the NPCs will not interact with you correctly for this specific path.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate Naobito at the Abandoned Shrine">
    Your journey begins at the Abandoned Shrine. You must approach the NPC Naobito while being Standless or having a non-JJK Spec equipped. Talking to him initiates the Zen'in clan dialogue, which sets you on the path of the clan's hierarchy.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Complete the Zen'in Progression Route">
    After speaking with Naobito, you will be tasked with proving your worth. This involves interacting with John Zen'in and completing a series of combat trials. These trials test your ability to handle multiple enemies without the crutch of high-tier Stands.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Conquer the Naoya Raid">
    The final step is the Naoya Raid. This is a specialized encounter where you must face the technical speed of the Zen'in style. Successfully clearing this raid grants you the necessary materials to finalize the Naoya Zen'in Spec.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finalize the Awakening">
    Once the raid is cleared and the requirements are met, return to the shrine to unlock the full potential of the Spec. This will grant you the "Head of Zen'in Clan" or "Zen'in Clan Member" titles depending on your progress.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Requirement">
Do not equip any JJK Spec (like Gojo or Sukuna) when starting the Naobito questline, or the progression route will fail to trigger. Use a Rokakaka Fruit if necessary to return to Standless.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Naoya Moveset and Technical Mechanics">

The core of **Sakura Stand Naoya** lies in its "Technical Rushdown" classification. The moveset is designed to keep opponents in a constant state of hitstun while providing the user with multiple escape and follow-up options. One of the most unique aspects of Naoya is the "G" variation system, which allows moves to change properties based on timing and directional inputs.

| Move Name | Type | Effect / Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Technical Strike** | Melee | Fast startup punch that leads into high-speed combos. |
| **Zen'in Speed** | Mobility | A dash that bypasses standard movement speed caps. |
| **G-Variation A** | Special | A technical counter that stuns the attacker for 1.5s. |
| **G-Variation B** | Special | A follow-up strike that scales with current combo count. |
| **Domain Expansion** | Ultimate | Reworked 2026 version with enhanced visual effects. |

The Spec thrives on aggressive pressure. By utilizing the Zen'in Speed dash, users can close the gap instantly, making it difficult for ranged Stands like Emperor to maintain distance. The 2026 balance pass also adjusted the damage scaling for Naoya, ensuring that while individual hits are lighter, the total combo potential is among the highest in the game.

<Callout type="info" title="Mastery Tip">
Naoya's performance is heavily tied to your Mastery level. At Mastery 1, the basic kit is unlocked, but the advanced G-variations and Domain refinements require higher Mastery tiers to be fully effective.
</Callout>
