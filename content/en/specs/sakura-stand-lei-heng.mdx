<Section icon={Map} title="How to Unlock the sakura stand lei heng Spec">

The **sakura stand lei heng** spec is one of the most mechanically unique and rewarding combat styles introduced in the Monster and Tiger Update. Inspired by Project Moon's universe, this spec requires players to participate in a high-stakes boss grind, manage a complex ammunition system, and master the art of low-health damage scaling. To unlock this powerful spec, you must follow a specific sequence of boss fights, item drops, and Stand requirements.

First, players must travel to the **Waterfall** area of the map. You can reach this location quickly by paying cash to the travel NPC. Once at the waterfall, your primary objective is to spawn the Toji Fushiguro boss. Every time Toji is summoned, there is a random chance that he will be replaced by **The Capo (Lei Heng)** boss instead.

Defeating The Capo is no easy task, as he features multiple aggressive phases. In his first phase, he utilizes rapid blue and purple slash skills. In his second phase, after a brief cutscene, he follows a strict attack pattern (Red -> Purple -> Green -> Blue). Finally, in his third phase, he enters an awakened state and loops unclashable Red skills and quick Blue slashes. You must dodge his Red skills (such as Tanglecleaver) entirely, as they cannot be clashed.

Upon defeating The Capo, there is a **40% chance** that he will drop the **Tiantui Star Blade** (often referred to as the Capo's Blade). Once you successfully obtain this weapon, you must equip the **Anubis Stand**. Ensure that your character has at least **60% Worthiness** before using the Tiantui Star Blade on Anubis. Doing so will consume the blade and permanently unlock the Lei Heng spec on your account. There is also a rare **1% chance** that the spec will unlock with its highly sought-after shiny variant, **Lei Heng | The 5th Sinner**.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Core Mechanics and Passive Abilities">

What sets the **sakura stand lei heng** spec apart from traditional rushdown specs is its reliance on high-risk passives and tactical ammo management. Instead of relying on raw cooldowns, players must monitor their health pool and ammunition to maximize their damage output.

### Inner Strength & The Shin Aura
The primary damage-scaling mechanic of Lei Heng is **Inner Strength**. As you take damage during combat, your offensive capabilities multiply. Specifically, after your character loses a total of **350 HP**, a distinct yellow aura known as **Shin [心]** will envelop your character model. This aura grants a flat **20% damage buff**. The buff can stack dynamically during prolonged engagements, allowing skilled players to reach a massive **4.8x damage multiplier** when fighting at low health.

### Unrelenting Spirit
To complement the risky nature of fighting at low health, the **Unrelenting Spirit** passive activates automatically when your health drops below **20%**. Upon activation, you receive a flat **15% defense buff** to help you survive critical strikes. Additionally, your standard keybinds undergo a shift: your Normal H move rebounds to Shin H+, and your Normal H+ move rebounds to Shin H, giving you instant access to your strongest finishers without needing complex inputs.

### Tigermark Ammunition
Lei Heng features a unique combat resource called **Tigermark Rounds**. You spawn with **12 Tigermark Rounds**. Every offensive ability in your moveset (excluding reloads) consumes these rounds. Managing this resource is vital because each remaining round in your chamber increases the damage of your core abilities, **Tanglecleaver** and **Savage Tigerslayer's Perfected Flurry of Blades**, by **+4.95**. Furthermore, as long as you have at least one Tigermark Round active, all of your basic attacks apply a persistent **burn effect** to your target.

### Savage Tigermark Rounds
By successfully executing your counter move (T), you instantly sacrifice all of your current standard rounds to gain **6 Savage Tigermark Rounds**. These specialized rounds are the exclusive requirement needed to unlock and cast your ultimate ability, Savage Tigerslayer's Perfected Flurry of Blades.

| Passive Name | Trigger Condition | Buff Effect |
| **Inner Strength** | Lose 350 HP total | **20% damage buff** (stacks up to **4.8x**) |
| **Unrelenting Spirit** | Health drops below 20% | **15% defense buff** and rebound H/H+ moves |
| **Tigermark Rounds** | Spawning / Reloading | **+4.95 damage** per round to key moves and burn effect |
| **Savage Rounds** | Successfully landing Counter (T) | **6 Savage Rounds** to unlock ultimate flurry |

<Callout type="tip" title="Ammunition Management">
Always keep an eye on your Tigermark Rounds. If you let your ammo drop to zero, you lose both your burn passive and the massive damage bonuses on your primary skills. Try to reload during safe windows when your opponent is knocked back or stunned.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Full Moveset and Keybinds Breakdown">

Mastering the **sakura stand lei heng** spec requires a deep understanding of its keybinds and how they interact with your ammunition pool. Because your moves consume rounds to deal maximum damage, blind button-mashing will quickly leave you vulnerable and out of ammo.

- **M1 Combo (Left Click)**: A rapid series of melee strikes that deal consistent damage and apply burn if you have active Tigermark Rounds. While fast, the M1 combo has relatively weak stun and cannot be easily chained into other moves without proper spacing.
- **Tanglecleaver (E)**: Lei Heng draws his blade for a heavy, sweeping slash. This move consumes your Tigermark Rounds to deal scaled damage. It is excellent for block-breaking and keeping aggressive opponents at bay.
- **Savage Tigerslayer's Perfected Flurry of Blades (H+ / Shin H)**: The ultimate ability of the spec. This move can only be executed when you have active **Savage Tigermark Rounds** (obtained via a successful counter). It unleashes an unavoidable flurry of high-damage slashes that shred the opponent's health bar.
- **Counter (T)**: Lei Heng enters a brief defensive stance. If an opponent strikes you during this window, you will block the attack, negate the damage, and instantly convert your current ammo pool into **6 Savage Tigermark Rounds**.
- **Reload (R)**: A brief channel that restores your standard pool of **12 Tigermark Rounds**. You are completely stationary and vulnerable to interrupts during the reload animation.

| Move Name | Keybind | Base Damage | Special Properties |
| **M1 Combo** | Left Click | **Varies** | Fast swings, low stun, cannot connect to other moves |
| **Tanglecleaver** | E | **Medium** | Consumes Tigermark Rounds, scales damage, inflicts burn |
| **Perfected Flurry** | H+ / Shin H | **High** | Requires Savage Rounds, massive multi-hit slash |
| **Counter** | T | **N/A** | Grants 6 Savage Rounds upon successful block |
| **Reload** | R | **N/A** | Restores 12 Tigermark Rounds, vulnerable during cast |

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Combat and Combo Strategy">

Because Lei Heng's moves do not naturally chain together as fluidly as rushdown specs like Garou, you must play deliberately. Your primary goal is to bait your opponent into hitting your counter, secure your Savage Rounds, and then punish them with your scaled damage multipliers.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Chip Damage and Burn Application">
    Begin the engagement by landing basic M1 strikes to apply the passive burn effect from your Tigermark Rounds. Do not overextend, as your M1s have low stun and cannot safely transition into heavy skills. Keep your distance and use the threat of your blade to control space.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Baiting the Counter">
    Once your opponent tries to retaliate with an aggressive rushdown combo, activate your Counter (T). Timing is crucial here; activating it too early makes it easily reactable, while activating it too late will result in you getting stunned. A successful block secures your 6 Savage Rounds.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Executing the Ultimate Flurry">
    With your Savage Rounds active, immediately unleash your ultimate, Savage Tigerslayer's Perfected Flurry of Blades (Shin H / H+). This multi-hit slash sequence deals massive damage that scales heavily if you have already lost health and activated your yellow Shin aura.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Safe Retreat and Reload">
    After executing your flurry, your opponent will be knocked back and temporarily stunned. Use this window of safety to dash backward, create distance, and press R to safely reload your standard 12 Tigermark Rounds. Do not attempt to reload while close to an active opponent, as you will be easily interrupted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Shin Aura Synergy">
Do not panic when your health drops. Lei Heng actually becomes significantly stronger at low health due to the **4.8x damage scaling** from Inner Strength and the extra defense from Unrelenting Spirit. Play patiently and let your passive buffs carry you to victory.
</Callout>

</Section>
