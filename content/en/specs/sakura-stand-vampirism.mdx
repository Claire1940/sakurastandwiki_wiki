<Section icon={Target} title="Best Combat Patterns and Priority">

Vampirism plays best when you control the pace instead of forcing every exchange. You want to create small decisions for the enemy: block, step back, or swing too early. Once that happens, your counter, freeze, and blood drain become much more threatening.

| Situation | Best Answer | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Enemy rushes in | G or Y | Stops pressure and creates space |
| You want to test range | T | Forces movement from a safer distance |
| Enemy whiffs a swing | E or R | Fast punish into follow-up pressure |
| You need recovery | H | Turns a confirmed opening into sustain |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Open with Space Control">
    Use T to test the opponent’s movement before you fully commit. This gives you information without giving up your position.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Punish Overextension">
    When an enemy swings too early, answer with G or Y. The goal is not just damage; it is to make future aggression expensive.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Convert the Opening">
    After a successful read, chain into your closer tools and finish with H if you can secure a safe confirm.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Reset the Fight">
    If the exchange gets messy, back out and reestablish space. Vampirism is strongest when you are choosing the next interaction.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Mistake">
Do not throw Bloodsuck early just because you are low. It is far better as a reward after you win neutral or catch a reckless push.
</Callout>
