<Section icon={Target} title="Prerequisites and Obtainment Mechanics">

Before you can even attempt to reach the Requiem state, you must fulfill several strict requirements. The most important factor is your **Worthiness** percentage. While you can attempt the ritual with lower worth, the risk is high: your Worthiness percentage directly correlates to your success rate. For instance, 50% Worthiness gives you a 50% chance of success.

| Requirement | Details | Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Base Stand** | Must have Anubis currently equipped | Mandatory |
| **Worthiness** | Recommended 100% (obtained from boxes or Chariot Requiem) | High |
| **Weather** | Must be a Thunderstorm (not just regular rain) | Mandatory |
| **Item** | Thunder Ring (spawns during Thunderstorms) | Mandatory |
| **Pity System** | Rain chance increases every 10 minutes until a storm occurs | Useful |

The weather system in Sakura Stand is the primary gatekeeper for this stand. Rain has a 1/8 chance to appear every 10 minutes in any season except Summer. Within that rain, there is a further 1/24 chance for it to escalate into a Thunderstorm.

<Callout type="warning" title="Worthiness Warning">
If you attempt the ritual and fail due to low Worthiness, the game will consume **1/8 of your total worth**. To avoid hours of wasted grinding, it is highly recommended to farm until you reach a guaranteed 100% Worthiness.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Ritual: Step-by-Step Obtainment">

Once you have your base Anubis and 100% Worthiness, you must wait for the skies to darken. The transition from rain to a storm is distinct, marked by heavy thunder and lightning. During this time, a **Thunder Ring** will spawn somewhere on the main map.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate the Thunder Ring">
    Search the main map during a Thunderstorm. The ring is a glowing interactable object. Ensure Anubis is your active stand before clicking it.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Enter the Dark Dimension">
    Upon interaction, you are transported to a pitch-black area. This is a scripted horror sequence. Strange noises, floating eyes, and chairs will appear as the area slowly lights up.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Complete the QTEs">
    As the ritual progresses, Quick-Time Events (QTEs) will appear on your screen. You must complete these successfully to survive the transition. Failing these may result in being kicked out of the dimension.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Final Slash">
    After roughly five minutes of the horror sequence, the dimension will appear to "break" with multiple slashes across the sky. You will then be teleported back to the main map with Anubis Requiem in your inventory.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
If you find the Thunderstorm spawn rate too frustrating, the Thunder Ring can also be dropped by the **Paper Curse: Final Boss**, though the drop rate is an abysmal 0.01%. Stick to the weather cycles for better odds.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mastery: Moveset and Passives">

The **sakura stand anubis requiem** is defined by its three unique passives. Unlike other stands that rely solely on damage, Anubis Requiem utilizes a "hit counter" system that rewards aggressive, consistent pressure.

| Passive Name | Effect Description | Tactical Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Inner Demon** | Hits add a blue counter; 30 hits = Instant Death | Forces enemies to play defensively |
| **Sharpest Blade** | Bypasses and removes enemy blocks on hit | Prevents turtling and defensive play |
| **Dimensional Damage** | Bypasses counters and most I-frame skills | Counters high-mobility and reactionary specs |

### Core Moveset (Base Form)

The base moveset focuses on dimensional manipulation and area denial. While the M1 swings are relatively slow, they are essential for stacking the Inner Demon passive.

| Key | Move Name | Description | Damage/Hits |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **LMB** | M1 Combo | A 4-hit slow swing combo | 10 Damage per hit |
| **E** | Dimension Corruption | Dashes back and creates a time bubble | 0 Base (16 in Awakening) |
| **R** | Dimensional Stab | A forward thrust that flips the blade | 16 Damage (+2 Hits) |
| **T** | Energy Slash | Slams the ground to create an exploding shockwave | 4-6 Shockwave Damage |
| **Y** | Dimensional Hop | Stabs the ground to trap enemies in a pocket dimension | Disables enemy skills |
| **H** | Dimension Slash | Large radius freeze that destroys the dimension | 0 Base (28 in Awakening) |
| **Z** | Gate | Teleportation move using specific phrases | Utility/Movement |

<Callout type="success" title="Combat Strategy">
Use **Dimensional Hop (Y)** to trap enemies who rely on high-cooldown skills. Once trapped and unable to use abilities, you can safely stack your M1 hits to trigger the **Inner Demon** execution.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Sanity Break: The Awakening">

By pressing **G**, the user enters the **Sanity Break** state. This is more than just a stat boost; it fundamentally changes the appearance and lethality of Anubis Requiem. The user grows horns, summons a second blade, and the iconic theme "Bury the Light" begins to play.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Enhanced Damage">
    Many moves that deal zero damage in base form (like Dimension Corruption) become lethal during Sanity Break.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Modified Moveset">
    The **R** move transforms into **Cross Blade**, a dual-slash that causes reality to break, dealing 16 damage and adding 2 hits to the counter.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dimensional Mastery">
    The **H** move (Dimension Slash) now deals a massive 28 damage in its radius, making it one of the strongest AOE attacks in the game.
  </Card>
  <Card title="The Penalty">
    When the awakening ends, the user falls to the ground for 3 seconds, leaving them completely vulnerable to counter-attacks.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip" title="Awakening Tip">
Only use Sanity Break when you are certain you can finish the fight or have enough distance to recover during the 3-second stun penalty at the end.
</Callout>
