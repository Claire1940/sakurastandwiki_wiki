<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Obtainment Route">

The main route is built around three checkpoints: the mask quest, the relic quest, and the invitation handoff. Every stage asks you to bring something concrete back to the NPCs, so the cleanest approach is to farm each objective in order instead of multitasking too early.

<Callout type="warning" title="Before You Start">
You need **Mastery 6** and **Standless** before the quest chain opens. Also, the Black Mask hunt is tied to a server with **10+ players**.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Talk to Olivia in the Dark Alley">
    Start the chain by speaking to Olivia. She sends you after the **Perception-Blocking Mask**, which is the first major gate.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Farm the Black Mask">
    Stay in a server with at least **10 players** and look for the black soul reveal. Once the target drops the mask, return it to Olivia.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Meet Harold near the campsite">
    After the mask handoff, go to the small forest campsite and complete Harold's quest by killing **10 players** and dealing **500 damage with Standless**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finish with the librarian">
    Bring Harold's relic to the librarian NPC near the cafe, finish the nine-weapon and laptop objectives, then accept the invitation and return to Olivia for the final reward.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| NPC | Location | Task | Reward |
|---|---|---|---|
| Olivia | Dark Alley | Start the mask quest | Progression begins |
| Harold | Campsite tent | Kill 10 players, deal 500 Standless damage | Smoldering Fragment |
| Librarian | Near the cafe | Weapon set and laptop objective | Library Invitation |
| Olivia | Final return | Complete the route | **The Black Silence** |

<Callout type="info" title="Routing Tip">
Do not rush the final handoff until you have every weapon and clue ready. The quest chain is easiest when you complete each gate in one farming session.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Moves, Phases, and Combo Flow">

Roland is not just about unlock pain; the spec also changes in a way that feels rewarding once you start using it. The core loop is built around hitting phase thresholds, opening better weapon strings, and ending with a cutscene move when you have cycled through the kit.

<Callout type="success" title="Combat Payoff">
After enough hits, the spec becomes noticeably more flexible. Phase changes improve range, expand move effects, and make your finisher easier to set up.
</Callout>

| Phase | Trigger | Main Change | Practical Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Base state | Standard weapon kit | Best for learning spacing |
| Phase 2 | After hit threshold | One skill improves | Better combo pressure |
| Phase 3 | After more hits | More range and control | Easier follow-ups |
| Phase 4 | After **45 hits** | New move behavior, taunt changes | Highest pressure and style |

| Move | Early Use | Later Use |
|---|---|---|
| Q / Basic chain | Weapon sequence | Remains your opener |
| Y | Core melee follow-up | Gains stronger handling in later phases |
| G | Slash pressure | Better with phase progression |
| H / Finisher | Cutscene-style ender | Requires prior move usage |

To get the most out of the spec, play it like a rhythm kit. Open with a safe poke, build your hits, then shift into the stronger phase tools once your opponent is forced to respect your range. The wiki also notes that the phase 4 theme and taunts change, which makes the final stage feel like a true upgrade rather than a simple damage buff.
