<Section icon={Map} title="Understanding the Sakura Stand Trading System">

Sakura Stand features a highly active trading economy, but its execution is vastly different from most Roblox experiences. Instead of clicking on a player or accessing a dedicated trading hub menu with a single button, you must utilize specific chat commands to initiate, accept, and finalize transactions. This design choice prevents accidental trades but can be confusing for new players who are unfamiliar with command-line mechanics.

The trading system is built to facilitate equal exchanges of Stands, Specs, skins, and rare progression items. Understanding the underlying structure, cooldowns, and interface options is essential to prevent losing high-value items to unfair deals.

| Metric | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| Core Command | !trade [Roblox Username] |
| Confirm Command | !confirm (used if UI button bugs out) |
| Decline Command | !decline or decline via UI |
| Minimum Mastery | Mastery 1 (requires reaching Level 50) |
| System Cooldown | 1 minute between transaction attempts |
| Cancel Window | 10 seconds to decline after accepting |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Always plan your transactions ahead of time. Because the system enforces a strict one-minute cooldown between trade attempts, minor typos or last-minute cancellations will force you to wait before trying again.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Trade in Sakura Stand: Step-by-Step">

Executing a trade successfully requires precision, as the system relies heavily on typing the correct details in the global chat menu. If you make a mistake with a player's name, the command will fail to execute. Follow this structured process to initiate and complete your trades safely.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Find a Trade Partner">
    Locate a player to trade with either in your current live server or through the trading channels in the official Sakura Stand Discord community. Agree on the items and values before initiating the command sequence.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Retrieve the Roblox Username">
    Hover over the player's in-game character or check the player list in the top-right corner of your screen. You must use their exact **Roblox Username**, not their customized display name or gaming tag.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Enter the Trade Command">
    Open the global chat window and type `!trade [Username]` (replacing [Username] with your partner's actual Roblox username). For example, if trading with a player named John, type `!trade John`.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Have your Partner Reciprocate">
    The other player must type the exact same command back to you, referencing your Roblox username (e.g., `!trade YourUsername`). Once both commands are registered by the server, the trading GUI window will automatically pop open on both screens.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Add Items and Confirm">
    Drag or click your items to place them into the trade slots. Double-check the other player's offer against community values. Click the green "Accept" button. If the UI button fails to register due to a common mobile display bug, type `!confirm` directly in the chat to bypass it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Scammers often use display names that look nearly identical to high-value traders while using completely different Roblox usernames. Always double-check the username in the trade window before clicking confirm.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Trading Requirements & Mastery Restrictions">

To protect the game's economy from botting, exploitation, and direct transfer abuse via alternative accounts, developers have implemented strict progression gates on the trading system. You cannot simply create a new Roblox account and immediately trade rare items to your main profile.

The baseline requirement to trade any item or currency is **Mastery 1**. To earn Mastery, you must reach the maximum level cap of 50, then open your settings menu and select "Upgrade Mastery". This resets your character's level back to 1 but increases your permanent Mastery level by 1. 

Furthermore, specific endgame Specs and Stands carry much higher Mastery requirements to prevent low-level accounts from holding overpowered abilities. Both the sender and the receiver must meet these specific Mastery thresholds to complete the exchange.

| Spec / Stand Name | Required Mastery to Trade | Trading Tier Class |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Cid Kagenou (Shadow) | Mastery 12 | High-Tier Spec |
| Sakuya | Mastery 12 | High-Tier Spec |
| Binah (The Arbiter) | Mastery 1 | High-Value Anchor |
| Sandevistan | Mastery 1 | Mid-Tier Spec |
| Ryomen Sukuna | Mastery 1 | Low-Tier Spec |

<Callout type="info" title="Mastery Retention">
If you perform a "Breakthrough" (which resets your level and mastery points once you accumulate 15 masteries), your unlocked trading privileges and inventory items remain completely intact.
</Callout>
