<Section icon={Target} title="How to Execute Trades: Commands and Steps">

Unlike many Roblox RPGs that feature a dedicated trading hub or a simple GUI button, Sakura Stand requires players to execute specific text commands in the global chat to initiate a trade. This manual system can be confusing for beginners, making it essential to follow the correct procedure to avoid errors.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Find the Target Player's Roblox Username">
    Hover your mouse cursor over the player's character or locate their name in the in-game player list in the top-right corner of your screen. **Crucial**: You must use their actual Roblox username, not their customized in-game display name.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Send the Trade Request via Chat">
    Open the global chat window and type the command: `!trade [username]` (replace `[username]` with the target player's exact Roblox username). Press Enter to send the request.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Wait for the Recipient to Accept">
    The other player must also open their chat and type the exact same command: `!trade [your_username]`. Once both players have executed the command pointing to each other, the secure trading window will automatically pop up on both screens.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Add Items and Verify the Value">
    Drag and drop the stands, specs, or skins you wish to offer into your side of the trading window. Carefully inspect the items the other player has put up. Ensure they match the agreed-upon trade and check their current placement on the **sakura stand trading tier list**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Finalize the Transaction Safely">
    Click the "Accept" button if you are satisfied with the offer. The game features a 10-second countdown period after both players accept. Use this time to do a final review; you can cancel the trade instantly at any point during this countdown if you suspect something is wrong.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Trading Safety and Scam Prevention">

Because Sakura Stand's trading system relies on chat commands and manual verification, scammers frequently target unsuspecting or impatient players. Protecting your hard-earned items requires strict adherence to safety protocols and a healthy dose of skepticism.

One of the most common scams involves "bait-and-switch" tactics, where a player quickly swaps a high-value skin for a visually similar but common variant right before accepting the trade. Always utilize the 10-second countdown window to double-check the exact names and details of the items in the trade box.
